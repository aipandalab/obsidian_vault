
## 1. 苦難與韌性 / Suffering and Resilience

**[00:00:02]**

I don't know how to teach it to you except for I hope suffering happens to you. One of my great advantages is that I have very low expectations. People with very high expectations have very low resilience. And unfortunately, resilience matters in success.

我不知道該怎麼把這個道理教給你們，但我希望你們都能經歷一些痛苦和磨難。我的一大優勢就是我的期望值很低。那些期望值過高的人，往往抗挫折的韌性很低。然而在通往成功的道路上，韌性至關重要。

**[00:00:13]**

Greatness is not intelligence, as you know. Greatness comes from character, and character isn't formed out of smart people—it's formed out of people who suffered.

你們知道，真正的偉大並非來自智商，而是來自性格。而優秀的性格並非在聰明人堆里隨隨便便形成的，它是在經歷痛苦和折磨的人身上磨礪出來的。

**[00:00:26]**

I'm about to tell you things I've never told anyone, not even my family. I was an immigrant, and when I came in 1973, I was nine. My older brother was almost 11, and this was a foreign country; there was nothing easy about that.

接下來我要告訴你們一些我從未對任何人提起過的事，甚至連我的家人都不知道。我曾是一名移民，1973年我來到這裡時只有 9 歲，我哥哥快 11 歲。對我們來說，這是一個完全陌生的國家，生活沒有一件事是容易的。

## 2. 把小事做到完美 / Lessons from Early Work

**[00:00:38]**

I had all kinds of jobs. We went to a school that included a lot of chores. I was the youngest kid in school, and so all of the other kids got the hard work and I got the easy job. I was 9 years old—I had to clean all the bathrooms. I never felt that I got the easy job, but that was my job. And so I did it delightfully, and then everything that I was doing, I wanted to do the best I could.

在學校里，我做過各種各樣的工作，我們的學校需要分擔很多家務雜事。因為我是學校里年齡最小的孩子，按理說那些大孩子應該分到重活，而我分到輕鬆的活。但當時 9 歲的我，被分到的任務是清理所有的洗手間。我從不覺得那是輕鬆的活，但那是我的工作，所以我就開開心心地把它做好了。從那時起，無論我做什麼，我都想盡自己最大的努力去做到最好。

**[00:01:01]**

My first job before CEO was a dishwasher, and I did that very well. I planned my work, I was organized—you know, I was _mise en place_—and then I washed the living daylights out of the dishes.

在我成為 CEO 之前，我的第一份工作是在 Denny's 餐廳當洗碗工。我做得非常好，我會規劃自己的工作，讓一切井然有序（Mise en place）。我把那些盤子刷得閃閃發亮。

**[00:01:13]**

And then, you know, they promoted me to busboy. I was certain I'm the best busboy Denny's ever had. You know, I never left the station empty-handed, I never came back empty-handed. I was very efficient. And so anyways, eventually, I became, you know, a CEO. I'm still working on being a good CEO.

後來，他們把我提升為收拾桌子的服務助理（Busboy）。我敢肯定，我是 Denny's 有史以來最優秀 的 Busboy。我從不空手離開工作站，也從不空手走回來，我的效率極高。就這樣，歷經磨練，我最終成為了一名 CEO。不過，至今我依然在努力學習如何成為一名優秀的 CEO。

## 3. 偉大工作的定義 / The True Definition of a Great Job

**[00:01:30]**

The thing is, when you want to build something great, it's not easy to do. And when you're doing something that's not easy to do, you're not always enjoying it. I don't love every day of my job. I don't think every day brings me joy, nor does joy have to be the definition of a good day. And every day I'm not happy, every year I'm not happy about the company, but I love the company every single second.

如果你想打造一些偉大的事業，這絕非易事。當你在做一件不容易的事情時，你不可能時刻享受其中。我不熱愛我工作的每一天，我不認為每一天都能給我帶來快樂，快樂也不應該成為“好日子”的唯一底線。並不是每一年我都對公司感到滿意，但我每分每秒都深愛著裝家公司。

**[00:01:54]**

So I think that what people misunderstand is somehow the best jobs are the ones that bring you happiness all the time. I don't think that that's right. You have to suffer, you have to struggle, you have to endeavor, you have to do those hard things and work through it in order to really appreciate what you've done. There is no such thing that is great that was easy to do.

人們常有一個誤解，認為最好的工作是那些能帶給裝源源不斷快樂的工作。我不這麼認為。你必須去經歷痛苦，去掙扎，去努力，去跨越那些艱難的坎坷，只有這樣，你才能真正珍惜你所取得的成就。世上沒有任何偉大的成就能夠隨隨便便成功。

**[00:02:21]**

I would say, therefore, I wish upon you greatness, which by my way of saying it—I wish upon you plenty of plain suffering.

因此，我祝願你們走向偉大。而按照我的理解，這意味著我祝願你們經歷足夠多的痛苦和折磨。

## 4. 拯救英偉達的命運 / Surviving the Near-Collapse of NVIDIA

**[00:02:27]**

But how does a dishwasher become one of the richest men on earth? Building a video company turned out to have been a million times harder than I expected it to be. If you want to do extraordinary things, it shouldn't be easy. I think that that's kind of the superpower of an entrepreneur: they don't know how hard it is, and they only ask themselves, _"How hard can it be?"_

但是，一個洗碗工是如何成長為世界上最富有的人之一的呢？製造晶片和發展視覺計算，其難度比我預想的要難上一百萬倍。如果你想成就非凡，它就不該是容易的。 think這正是創業者的“超能力”所在：他們不知道這條路到底有多難，他們轉頭只會問自己——“這能有多難？”

**[00:02:50]**

To this day, I trick my brain into thinking, _"How hard can it be?"_ You have to get yourself to believe that it's not that hard, because it's way harder than you think.

直到今天，我依然在用這句話來“欺騙”我的大腦。你必須讓自己相信它沒有那麼難，因為現實往往比你想象的要難得多。

**[00:02:56]**

At NVIDIA, I experienced failures—great big ones, all humiliating and embarrassing. Many nearly doomed us. NVIDIA was almost out of money. Jensen had just taken the biggest contract in the company's history, but he quickly realized something: if they kept going, NVIDIA would head in the wrong direction. But if they walked away from the deal, the company might not survive either. Way we would be out of business.

在英偉達（NVIDIA），我經歷過巨大的失敗，每一次都讓人感到羞辱和尷尬，許多次甚至讓公司瀕臨絕境。當時，英偉達幾乎耗盡了資金。我剛剛簽下了公司歷史上最大的一筆合同（與世嘉 Sega 的合作），但很快我就意識到，我們的技術路線走錯了。如果我們繼續走下去，英偉達將誤入歧途；但如果我們單方面終止合同，公司就無法生存。無論走哪條路，我們都難逃倒閉的命運。

**[00:03:28]**

I contacted the CEO of Sega and explained that our invention was the wrong approach, we had to stop. But I needed Sega to pay us in whole, or NVIDIA would be out of business. I was embarrassed to ask the CEO of Sega. To his credit, and my amazement, he agreed. His understanding and generosity gave us six months to live. With that, we built RIVA 128 just as we were running out of money, and saved the company. Confronting our mistake and, with humility, asking for help saved NVIDIA.

於是，我頂著壓力聯繫了世嘉的 CEO，向他解釋我們的發明是錯誤的方向，我們必須停止。但我請求世嘉能夠全額支付我們這筆款項，否則英偉達就會倒閉。提出這個請求讓我感到無比羞愧。但令我驚訝和感激的是，世嘉的 CEO 展現了極大的理解與慷慨，他同意了。這筆錢給了我們 6 個月的生存時間。憑藉著這最後的喘息之機，我們在資金即將見底時成功研發出了 RIVA 128 顯卡，從而拯救了公司。直面錯誤並帶著謙遜請求幫助，拯救了英偉達。

**[00:04:04]**

I learned early that being CEO is not about power, but the responsibility that comes with keeping the company alive. We learned never to see failure as the opposite of success. Each failure is just another learning moment, a humility moment, a character-strengthening moment. The resilience forged through setbacks is what gives you the strength to go again.

我很早就學到，成為 CEO 職位並不意味著擁有權力，而是意味著承擔維持公司生存的責任。我們學會了從不將失敗視為成功的對立面。每一次失敗都只是另一個學習的機會、一個保持謙遜的時刻、一個重塑性格的時刻。在挫折中鑄就的韌性，才是讓裝能夠重新站起來的力量源泉。

## 5. 堅守核心信念 / Holding Onto Core Beliefs

**[00:04:33]**

In 2008, NVIDIA's stock crashed nearly 80%. Very few people knew this company would one day become the most valuable in the world. So, how did Jensen make it through?

2008年，英偉達的股價暴跌了近 80%。當時幾乎沒有人能預料到，這家公司有一天會成為世界上最具價值的企業。面對這樣的困境，我是如何熬過來的？

**[00:04:46]**

When your share price drops 80%, it's a little embarrassing. You don't want to get out of your bed, you don't want to leave the house. All of that is true. But then, you go back to just doing your job.

當你的股價下跌 80% 時，確實挺讓人難堪的。你甚至不想起床，不想出門。這都是真實的心境。但接下來，你還是得回到工作本身。

**[00:04:55]**

I woke up at the same time, I prioritized my day in the same way, always gut-checked back to the core: What do you believe? What are the most important things? Family loves me, okay, check. And so you just got to check it off and then go back to work.

我依然在同一時間醒來，以同樣的方式規劃我一天的優先級，總是重新審視核心問題：你到底相信什麼？什麼才是最重要的事情？家人依然愛我，很好，確認這一點。然後，繼續回去工作。

**[00:05:09]**

Do you believe in it? Did something change? The stock price changed, but did something else change? Did physics change? Did gravity change? Did all of the things that we assumed, that we believed that led to our decision—did any of those things change? Because if those things change, you got to change everything. But if none of those things change, you change nothing. Keep on going.

你還相信你的事業嗎？有什麼東西改變了嗎？改變的只是股票價格。但其他東西變了嗎？物理定律變了嗎？引力變了嗎？我們做決策所基於的那些核心假設和信念改變了嗎？因為如果那些根本性的東西變了，你就必須改變一切；但如果它們沒有變，你就什麼都不用變，繼續堅持下去。

**[00:05:26]**

You do things based on core beliefs. We deeply believe that we could create a computer that solves problems normal processing can't do. You know, have a core belief, gut-check it every day, pursue it with all your might, pursue it for a very long time, surround yourself with people you love, and take them on that ride. That's the story of NVIDIA.

你的所作所為應當基於你的核心信念。我們深信，我們可以創造出一種能夠解決傳統計算無法解決的複雜問題的計算機。你需要擁有一個核心信念，每天在內心深處拷問並堅守它，用盡全力去追求它，堅持很長很長的時間，並與你熱愛的人同行。這就是英偉達的故事。

## 6. 面對 AI 時代的生存法則 / Navigating the AI Revolution

**[00:05:47]**

AI is changing every industry on Earth, and Jensen thinks most people still don't understand what's coming.

AI 正在改變地球上的每一個行業，而我認為大多數人依然沒有真正意識到即將到來的是什麼。

**[00:05:54]**

You're not going to lose your job to AI; you're going to lose your job to somebody who uses AI. Your company is not going to go out of business because of AI; your company is going to go out of business because another company used AI. You have no choice but to get engaged as quickly as you can. Don't fall behind. You don't have to be the first company to take advantage of AI, but don't be the last.

你不會因為 AI 而失去工作，但你可能會輸給一個善用 AI 的人；你的公司不會因為 AI 而倒閉，但可能會被另一家善用 AI 的公司淘汰。你別無選擇，必須儘可能快地參與進來，不要掉隊。你不一定要成為第一家吃螃蟹的公司，但千萬不要成為最後那一個。

**[00:06:13]**

Human language, by the way, is the best programming language of the future. You all know how to prompt the computer to make it do things. You're at the beginning, at the starting line of AI. What will you create? Whatever it is, run after it like we did. Run, don't walk. Remember, either you're running for food, or you are running from becoming food. Either way, run.

順便提一句，人類語言將成為未來最好的編程語言。你們都知道如何向計算機發出指令（Prompt）來讓它做事。我們正處於 AI 時代的起跑線上。你打算創造些什麼？無論那是什麼，像我們當年一樣，奔跑著去追尋它吧！用跑的，不要用走的。記住：要么為了尋找食物而奔跑，要么為了不成為別人的食物而奔跑。無論如何，跑起來！

## 7. 將工作視為一生的志業 / Work as Your Life's Work

**[00:06:35]**

After all the failures, all the suffering, and all the success, Jensen's message is surprisingly simple: find work that gives you great joy, or just fall in love with whatever work you do. That's the only way you're going to do your best work. That's the only way you're going to do exquisite work. Instead of working for a living, think of it as your life's work.

歷經所有的失敗、痛苦與成功，我的核心信息其實極其簡單：找到能帶給你巨大快樂的工作，或者，徹底愛上你當下正在做的任何工作。這是你能夠把工作做到極致的唯一途徑，也是你能夠做出精湛成就的唯一方法。不要僅僅為了謀生而工作，把工作當成你一生的志業。

**[00:07:03]**

I wish for you to love your work as much as I've loved my work. Lori, my kids, my family, the nearly 6,000 employees at NVIDIA, and everybody I've ever worked with in the computer industry knows I love my work. NVIDIA is my life's work.

我希望你們能夠像我熱愛我的工作一樣去熱愛你們的工作。我的 my 妻子、我的孩子們、我的家人，以及英偉達近 6000 名員工，還有每個在計算機行業與我共事過的人都知道，我熱愛我的工作。英偉達就是我一生的志業。

**[00:07:16]**

I work from the moment I wake up to the moment I go to bed, and I work seven days a week. When I'm not working, I'm thinking about working. I'm obsessed about GPUs. I mean, what can you do? Our purpose—almost all of our purposes—should be to go and do something that has never been done before, that is insanely hard to do, that if you achieve it, could make a real contribution.

我從醒來的那一刻一直工作到上床睡覺，我一周工作七天。當我沒有在實際工作時，我的大腦也在思考工作。我甚至對 GPU 到了痴迷的程度。我們幾乎所有人的目標，都應該是去嘗試做一些從未有人做過的事情，那些極其困難的事情。一旦你實現了它，就能為世界做出真正的貢獻。

**[00:07:35]**

I do every single morning exactly the same way. I begin each morning by doing my highest priority work first. I have a very clear priority list, and I start from the highest priority work first. Before I even get to work, my day is already a success.

我每天早晨的流程完全一樣。我總是從做最高優先級的工作開始。我有非常清晰的優先級清單。在動身去公司之前，我這一天其實就已經算得上成功了。

**[00:07:53]**

It is the most extraordinary thing, Bill, that a normal dishwasher, busboy could grow up to be this. There's no magic—it's just 61 years of hard work every single day. I don't think there's anything more than that.

最不可思議的是，一個普普通通的洗碗工、Busboy，能夠一路成長為今天的樣子。這其中沒有任何魔法，僅僅是 61 年來，每一天都在腳踏實地地努力工作。我不認為還有什麼別的秘訣。

## 8. 超越智商的終極超能力 / Superpowers of Endurance

**[00:08:08]**

More importantly, we developed agility and a culture of resilience. One setback after another, we shook it off and skated to the next opportunity. Each time, we gained skills and strengthened our character. Our company is really hard to distract and really hard to discourage. No setback that comes our way doesn't look like an opportunity these days.

更重要的是，我們在一次又一次的挫折中，培養出了敏捷性與堅韌的公司文化。我們抖落身上的灰塵，滑向下一個機會。每一次，我們都獲得了新的技能，強健了我們的性格。我們的公司極難被干擾，也極難被挫敗。如今在我們看來，任何降臨的挫折都更像是一個機遇。

**[00:08:33]**

I hope you believe in something—something unconventional, something unexplored. But let it be informed and let it be reasoned, then dedicate yourself to making it happen. You may find your GPU, you may find your CUDA, you may find your generative AI, you may find your NVIDIA.

我希望你們能夠相信一些東西——一些反傳統的、未被探索的事物。但請讓它是理性的、經過推導的，然後全力以赴去實現它。你可能會找到屬於你的 GPU，屬於你的 CUDA，屬於你的生成式 AI，屬於你自己的“英偉達”。

**[00:08:55]**

I hope you will see setbacks as new opportunities. Your pain and suffering will strengthen your character, your resilience, and agility—and they are the ultimate superpowers of all.

我希望你們能將挫折視為新的機遇。你的痛苦和重重磨難將塑造你的性格、你的韌性與敏捷度，而這些，才是終極的超能力。

**[00:09:03]**

Of the things that I value most about my abilities, intelligence is not top of that list. My ability to endure pain and suffering, my ability to work on something for a very, very long period of time, my ability to handle setbacks and see the opportunity just around the corner—I consider to be my superpowers. And I hope they're yours.

在我所看重的個人能力中，智商絕非名列前茅。我忍受痛苦和折磨的能力，我長時間專注於某件事的能力，以及我應對挫折並敏銳看到轉角處機會的能力——我將這些視作我的超級能力。我希望它們也能成為你們的超級能力。

**[00:09:24]**

And I hope you find a craft. It's not important to decide on day one, it's not even important to decide anytime soon, but I hope you do find a craft that you want to dedicate your lifetime to perfecting. And let it be your life's work.

我希望你們能找到一門值得熱愛的手藝。第一天就決定它並不重要，甚至在短期內沒決定也不要緊。但我希望你們最終能找到一門願意用一生去追求完美的技藝，讓它成為你一生的志業。

**[00:09:42]**

Do you like my jacket? This is what my wife bought for me, and this is what I'm wearing. To me, no task is beneath me, because remember, I used to be a dishwasher. And I mean that.

（笑）你們喜歡我的夾克嗎？這是我妻子幫我買的，所以我一直穿著它。對我而言，沒有任何工作是微不足道的，因為請記住，我曾是一名洗碗工。我是認真的。
